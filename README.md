# superstore-sales-analysis
## Project Overview
Analysis of 9,994 retail transactions from a US Superstore (2014–2017) 
to identify category profitability gaps, regional performance anomalies, 
and sub-category inefficiencies to inform business planning decisions.

## Business Questions
1. Which category generates the highest revenue and profit?
2. Which region has the highest sales but lowest profit margin?
3. What is the monthly sales trend across 2017 and which quarter performs best?
4. Which customer segment drives the most revenue and highest average order value?
5. Which sub-categories are generating sales volume but recording a loss?

## Key Findings
- Technology leads in both revenue ($3.49M) and profit ($145K), while 
  Furniture generates comparable revenue ($3.23M) but only $18K in profit, 
  indicating a cost structure problem requiring urgent review.
- Central is the only loss-making region with a -10% average profit margin 
  despite 2,323 sales — the highest priority area for operational intervention.
- Tables, Bookcases, and Supplies are actively destroying value, generating 
  combined losses of -$22,387 despite 737 total units sold.
